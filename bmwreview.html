Intro

The BMW 6-series used to sit at the top of the very complicated range. It doesn't anymore...it's gone... with one exception. The big coupe in the family is now the BMW 850i. V8 engine, lots of luxury, the spiritual success of the classic 3.0 litre 850i in the early 1970s.

Driving BMW 850i

The basics here... We got turbocharged V8 5.0 litre engine, 8-speed transmission, Xdrive. When you are in sport mode it feels more rear wheel drive than just 4-wheel drive. The thing is for a car that would be the flagship sports car of the 8-series range it feels pretty quick. 3.7s to 100km. For a car that's 1960 kilograms thats pretty impressive.

Dashboard

This and the Z4 are the first BMWs to get the full adaptive digital dashboard and it works pretty well even though the backwards tachometer takes a little bit to used to.

Interior

Inside we are driving with luxury. Car play, android auto, inductive charging and lots of additional cool features but for all of them you have to pay extra of course because this is BMW and every additional extra is paid. The backseats are very tiny and you have to be near 1.60cm tall to fit in the back. But for a small children its comfortable. The front seats are super comfortable with a lot of features There are a lot of options for the front seats: heated, ventilated, massaging, memory and more. The leather is nice to touch and the stitches make the car look aggresive from the inside.

Conclusion

You can feel the accuracy, the directness when you put your foot on the gas pedal, but the feedback is lacking... which is kind of disapointing because the rest of the car for a vehicle with this much weight is actually pretty evolving. You can adjust this car in anyway you like: on the throttle, on the brake, on the steering, especially in the sport mode. Its everything really. But if you going to have a car that you just want to enjoy the design this is your car.
